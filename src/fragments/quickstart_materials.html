Quickstart Materials

No crafting system is complete without a selection of crafting materials. But how do you build new, flavorful materials for your game?

This chapter introduces quickstart materials. Combine these names with a rarity and essence to create unique crafting materials for your game fast.

Creating a Material

To create a crafting material for your game, follow these three simple steps:

  1. Choose a name: Pick a name for your material based on it's type—chemical, creative, curio, durable, edible, or fabric. Use the Monstrous Materials tables as a starting point for inspiration.
  2. Assign a rarity: Assign a rarity to your material based on the CR of the source—the higher the CR, the rare the material.
  3. Assign an essence: Pick an essence for your material based on the thematic source.

Krazak has just survived a near-fatal fight with a beholder in the Writhing Caverns. He takes a moment to survey the corpse...

  • Krazak:Hah, I knew that beholder wasn't up to scratch. Now let's cut it open. What can I harvest?
  • DM:As a master builder, Krazak, you think you can harvest... (checks the tables) ...some sticky bile from the beholder using your smith's tools. This is a very rare, aberrant, durable material.
  • Krazak:Ah perfect—that's just what I need to finish off my warpslayer axe. Right kids, let's get to hacking.

Earth Earth, nature, hardy, heavy, stubborn, reliable.

Radiant Light, cleansing, restoration, calming, ordered.
Water Water, cold, slippery, shifting, tempestuous, chaotic.

Material Units

Material measurements are abstracted into "units". One unit of material is a complete thing and can't be subdivided.
