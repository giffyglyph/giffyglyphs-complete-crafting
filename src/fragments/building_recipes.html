Quickstart Recipes

Before you can craft a magic item, you need a crafting recipe. But how do you create new, flavorful recipes for your game?

This chapter introduces quickstart recipes and advice on how to create your own crafting recipes fast.

Creating a Recipe

To create a crafting recipe for your game, follow these three basic steps:

  1. Choose a Profession and Tool: Pick one profession and one tool that is required to use the recipe.
  2. Choose a Rarity: Decide how rare your item is—the rarer the item, the harder it is to craft.
  3. Choose a Power and Utility: Decide how powerful your item is and how often it can be used.

  4. Assign Cores and Materials: Use the Magic Items tables to see how many crafting cores and crafting materials your recipe should require.

    Combine these with quickstart materials to generate a set of lore-friendly components.

  5. Calculate any Arcane Dust: If your item needs to be shaped into a particular form, calculate how much arcane dust is required for the recipe.
  6. Set a Crafting Time: Finally, decide how long it takes to craft your item—the more powerful the item, the longer it will take.

Adjust Where Necessary

These tables assume a standard level of item power and crafting ability. Depending on the specifics of your item and world, you may want to increase or decrease some requirements to better suit the power level of your game.

AItem Power

Magic items come in all shapes and sizes with a boundless range of special powers—flaming swords, teleporting armor, life-giving potions, homing arrows, etc.

These items typically fall into one of two broad categories—minor items and major items:

  • Minor: Most consumables (i.e single or limited use items) and situational utility items.
  • Major: Items that provide a significant combat or social benefit—weapons, armor, attack bonuses, summoning creatures, altering ability scores, etc.

Use your best judgement and choose the category that best suits the power level of your item.

BItem Utility

Magic items vary in their utility—some have a fixed number of applications before they expire, while others have long-lasting or permanent effects.

There are four categories of item utility: single, limited, charged, and permanent.

  • Single: This item can be used only once before it expires or is consumed.
  • Limited: This item can be used a fixed number of times before it expires or is consumed.
  • Charged: This item has a set number of uses that recharge after a period of time.
  • Permanent: This item has a permanent effect that doesn't diminish under normal circumstances.

Choose the utility that best describes the features of your magic item. If your item has multiple features (i.e. a limited and a permanent feature), use the higher grade.

CArcane Dust

When you craft a magic item, arcane dust is used to help shape that item into a particular form—a breastplate for a +1 breastplate, a longsword for a frost brand longsword, a sheet of paper for a spell scroll, etc.

To find out how much arcane dust your recipe requires:

  1. Choose a Shape: Choose a mundane (i.e. non-magical) item as the fundamental form for your magic item.
  2. Calculate the Cost: Translate the gp cost of the mundane item into units of arcane dust—the higher the rarity of the magic item, the more arcane dust required.
  3. Update the Recipe: Add the dust as a crafting requirement to your recipe.

Mundane Items

To craft a mundane (i.e. non-magical) item, you don't require a specific crafting recipe—just a relevant crafting tool and materials.

You may use any combination of crafting materials equal to—or exceeding—the common value of the mundane item.

Profession Requirements

Some mundane items require a level of professional expertise to craft. Achieve higher ranks in your profession to craft mundane items of greater value.

Crafting Time

The time required to craft a mundane item depends on the effort: trivial (0.5 days), minor (2 days), or major (7 days).

Choose an effort that makes most sense for your item by taking into account size, complexity, and value.
