Getting Started

Even the most bloodthirsty barbarian will, on occassion, be compelled to craft something—even if it's just a bigger, bloodthirstier sword. When players want to harvest the skin of a slain dragon, or sew a Robe of Many Eyes, or cook a Heroes Feast, Giffyglyph's Complete Crafting has you covered.

But what are the general concepts of crafting and how can you easily introduce them into your game? This chapter will guide you through the basics and outline some introductory levers to get you started.

The Basics

This supplement introduces six basic crafting concepts: professions, cores, materials, nodes, recipes, and goods. Characters use professions to gather materials from nodes and follow recipes to craft them into goods.

  1. Profession: The ability to read recipes and use tools to manufacture goods and harvest materials.
  2. Core: A power source for a magical good.
  3. Material: A resource used to craft goods.
  4. Node: A source of crafting materials—a monster's corpse, a vibrant plant, a metal vein.
  5. Recipe: An instructional blueprint that tells you how to craft a specific good from materials.
  6. Good: A finished item or effect.

Viridian is an apprentice artist with training in painter's tools. After he defeats an ogre zombie, Viridian extracts a measure of luminescent ichor (common, creative, necrotic) from the corpse.

The ichor makes for an effective paint and Viridian uses it to craft a fine painting for his room.

1Professions

Crafting professions teach you how to use crafting recipes and harvest crafting materials.

You can pursue any number of six crafting professions, each with five ranks of mastery—apprentice, expert, artisan, master, and grandmaster. By achieving higher ranks in your profession, you can craft more exclusive recipes and harvest legendary materials.

For a full exploration of professions, see page pX.

Learning a Profession

Different professions use different tools for their trade. If you are proficient with at least one set of crafting tools, you can be trained in the relevant profession.

Once you have attained a basic understanding of a crafting tool, you can learn a profession in one of three primary ways:

  1. Join a Guild: Crafting guilds are special (sometimes secret) organisations where crafters train, practice, socialise, and share knowledge about their craft. If you can earn an invite into a guild, you may have access to the best tools and techniques.

    But be careful—these guilds may have strange laws and traditions about where, when, and how you can ply your craft. It's a bad idea to cross your guild...

  2. Find a Mentor: Crafting mentors can offer one-on-one tuition in a particular craft—for a sizable fee, of course.

    But be careful—mentors can be hard to impress. You may have to perform a notable task to earn your mentor's approval.

  3. Read a Manual: Crafting manuals contain written knowledge, advice, formulae, and tutorials to help you master professional techniques. This may be the slowest way to learn a profession, but it remains an option for the lightest of pockets.

    But be careful—not all manuals are equal. Some manuals are stolen from their author—some may even be fakes with deliberately falsified formulae.

2Cores

A crafting core is a shard of raw magical power that is used to transform mundane materials into magical goods.

Cores come in one of five rarities—common, uncommon, rare, very rare, and legendary. You need at least one crafting core to craft a magical item, so hunt far-and-wide for these elusive components.

Finding Cores

During your travels, you can discover crafting cores in three primary ways:

  1. Go on Adventures: Cores are coveted by crafters and creatures alike. Journey into the dark places of the world to recover them.
  2. Receive a Boon: Perform a service for a notable patron and you may be gifted with a core in return.
  3. Spend Gold: Some settlements may have a very limited number of cores for sale. But act fast—other crafters will be sure to buy them if you don't.

Viridian wants to craft a set of magical Marvelous Pigments—but the recipe requires a very rare crafting core. Rumors abound that the necromancer Deserad d'Eath has at least one lucent core in his possession...

Cores are Treasure

Cores are the key to crafting magical items. These special cores can't be crafted or bought through normal means—instead, they must be hunted for through adventures, quests, and campaigns.

Use cores to limit how many magic items are in your game—the more cores you introduce, the more magic items the players can craft. As a general rule of thumb:

  • Major Reward: When you would give a major magic item as a reward, give 5 crafting cores instead.
  • Minor Reward: When you would give a minor magic item as a reward, give 2 crafting cores instead.

3Materials

Crafting materials are resources that—when combined with cores and recipes—can be turned into goods.

Each crafting material has a rarity ("how special am I?"), a type ("what am I best used for?"), and an essence ("what innate power do I represent?").

These three details—rarity, type, and essence—tell you everything important about a material and guide how it can be used in crafting recipes. Gather the rarest of materials to craft the rarest of recipes.

Exchanging Materials

Materials are flexible. Names and sources are (in most cases) entirely superficial—what truly matters in crafting are the three material tags: rarity, type, and essence.

When a recipe asks for a material, you can use any material that matches these three tags. You can also substitute a material of higher rarity for a lower one—using a rare material in place of a common one, for example.

Truth wants to craft a Frost Brand longsword. The recipe asks for 12 frostbitten crystal ores (very rare, durable, water).

Truth has only 7 ores—but he has 5 shards of rainbow ice (very rare, durable, water). The tags match, and so he can use these materials interchangably.

Finding Materials

During your travels, you can collect crafting materials in four primary ways:

  1. Hunt Monsters: Monsters are a prime source of crafting materials. Corrosive oozes, fiery dragon bones, monstrous meats—hunt legendary monsters in your adventures to craft legendary goods.
  2. Discover Nodes: Metal veins, hardy trees, stone quarries, fresh lakes—harvest crafting materials from rich nodes out in the wilds.
  3. Salvage Goods: With the right tools, you may be able to salvage some materials when you destroy a good.
  4. Spend Gold: Some settlements may have a limited number of materials for sale. Try looking for artificers, gatherers, merchants, and crafting guilds—but act fast! Other crafters will be sure to buy them if you don't.

Aberinyth Swine is a penny-pinching merchant in the village of Little Westing. Each week, he has a fresh stock of 50 assorted common materials available to buy at 1.5 gp each.

There is an 80% chance that these materials will all be sold by the third day of the week.

4Nodes

Materials are extracted from crafting nodes. These nodes come in all shapes and sizes—tiny monsters, gargantuan dragons, metal veins, crop fields—and the bigger the node, the more attempts you can make to harvest materials.

Hunt the biggest monsters and—with the right tools and training—harvest them for your own crafting needs.

5Recipes

A crafting recipe tells you how to combine cores and materials to create a good—a flaming magical sword, a fine wooden chair, a delicate ruby necklace, etc. Recipes require a profession—you must have the right training and tools to perform this craft.

Arcane Dust

Some recipes require a measure of arcane dust. This special powder—created from crushed gemstones—is used to help shape and infuse magical power into a crafted object. You can buy arcane dust from willing artificers.

Finding Recipes

Before you can use a recipe, you must have it recorded somewhere close to hand—typically in your recipe book. During your travels, you can discover crafting recipes in three primary ways:

  1. Be Trained: When you receive training in a profession and gain a new rank, you learn 3 crafting recipes. You may also be able to pay mentors for some additional recipe-specific training.
  2. Experiment: You can run experiments during your downtime to try and discover a specific recipe.
  3. Read a Recipe Book: Buy—or discover—a recipe book and read it to learn new recipes.

Record your recipes in your own recipe book and keep it close to hand—a crafter's recipe book is a valuable thing, and many a careless crafter has found their priceless formulae stolen by a light-fingered thief.

6Goods

A crafted good is what you produce from a recipe. Goods typically fall into one of three broad categories:

  • Items: a magic sword, a cursed ring, a fine meal, etc.
  • Effects: an explosion, some healing, a circle of banishment, etc.
  • Structures: a hut, a house, a tavern, a castle, etc.

For a full exploration of goods, see page pX.
