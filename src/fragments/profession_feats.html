Profession Feats

To craft special items, you must first take on a crafting profession. Through careful study and with regular practice, you can master profession feats to expand your crafting potential.

This chapter details a variety of feats for each crafting profession and rank. Introduce these into your game to get started with professions fast.

Earning a Feat

A character can earn a profession feat in one of three primary ways:

  • Join a Guild: Crafting guilds are special (sometimes secret) organisations where crafters train, practice, socialise, and share knowledge about their craft.
  • Train with a Mentor: Crafting mentors can offer one-on-one tuition in a particular profession.
  • Study a Manual: Crafting manuals contain written knowlege, advice, formulae, and tutorials to help you master professional techniques.

Use a variety of these methods as best suits your campaign. As a general rule-of-thumb, player should expect to have access to certain profession ranks once they start to reach certain character levels:

Player Profession Ranks

Character Level Rank

Weaver's Guild of Upper Thal

  • Crafting
  • Profession
  • Tailor
  • Guild

The Weaver's Guild of Upper Thal is the place to learn the fine art of tailoring. You can purchase a guild application for 100 GP—but you must find a ranked guildmember to sponsor your application before it can be accepted.

Within its walls, you can petition guildmembers for training and tuition. But beware—Guildmaster Silk tolerates no timewasters nor tretchery. Perform services to the guild to stay in Silk's good favour and open the doors to higher ranks of tailoring tuition.

Gastron d'Nom

  • Crafting
  • Profession
  • Culinarian
  • Mentor

A well-known hedge chef, Gastron d'Nom wanders the realm seeking new culinary delights and challenging other chefs in his famous Great Grand Cook-Off competition.

If you can convince Gastron to take you on as a student—and if you can best him in the Great Grand Cook-off—you may be able to learn the ways of a Master Culinarian...

A Basic Guide To Building

  • Crafting
  • Profession
  • Builder
  • Manual

This tattered manual—handed down carefully through five generations of the Strongsmithy clan—contains the very basics of building techniques as first taught by the overly-articulate Pebble Breist von Strongsmithy III.

If you spend 1 week studying this manual and spend 25 GP in crafting practice, you can make an Intelligence check (DC 15). After four successes, you earn the Apprentice Builder feat.
